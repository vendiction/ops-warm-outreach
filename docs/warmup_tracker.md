## Graduation criteria — the "ready to send" bar

An account flips 🟡 → 🟢 **only when all four hold.** This is the gate Phase 7 respects; do not
send from an account that hasn't cleared it, regardless of the calendar.

1. **≥30 days** of logged activity (45 preferred for LinkedIn — it's the most aggressive detector).
2. **No action-block, checkpoint, or challenge** in the final 7 days.
3. **Minimum footprint:** ≥100 follows, ≥300 likes, ≥40 genuine comments, ≥8 original posts.
4. **A profile that looks lived-in:** avatar, bio, 8+ posts, some inbound follows/engagement.

Jon signs off on the first account of each platform before any send. After that, Julia can
graduate accounts against this checklist.

## Daily activity target (per account, ~10–20 min)

- Log in via the Dolphin profile (never a raw browser).
- Follow 5–10 accounts in the ICP niche (info/coaching/course space).
- Like 15–30 posts.
- Comment on 2–5 posts — real, specific text. No "🔥🔥" or "great post!".
- Post original content 2–3×/week.
- **No DMs.**

## Attrition & the re-warm queue

Account loss is expected, not exceptional — **assume ~monthly churn per platform.** Standing rule:

- Keep **at least one spare per platform** always in warming, so a 🔴/⚫ account can be swapped
  in without halting that channel.
- When an account goes 🟠 cooldown: pause it 72h, resume at half activity, watch for a second
  signal. Two signals in a week → treat as 🔴.
- When an account goes 🔴 restricted or ⚫ burned: log the date + suspected cause below, start a
  fresh persona's 30–45 day clock immediately, do not reuse the proxy session.

## LinkedIn caveat — escalate before relying on it

LinkedIn detects and litigates automation more aggressively than IG/FB, and automated persona
DMs are a direct ToS break. Of the three channels it's the one most likely to burn accounts
fast. **Confirm with Jon whether LinkedIn stays in the send mix before graduating LI personas
to 🟢.** If it stays, keep LI daily caps at the bottom of the Phase 7 range and expect higher churn.
